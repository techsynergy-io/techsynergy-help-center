# Negotiate Terms with Clients

**Who this is for:** Freelancers

## What you'll achieve

- Confidently agree on fair project terms with clients
- Set clear expectations around scope, pricing, and delivery before work begins

## Prerequisites

- An active Freelancers account
- A client interested in working with you
- Clear understanding of your skills, availability, and desired rates

## Why Negotiation Matters

Negotiating terms helps protect your time, value, and working relationship. Clear agreements reduce misunderstandings, prevent scope creep, and ensure both you and the client are aligned before the project starts.

## Steps

- **Understand the project requirements**
  *Expected result:* You know exactly what the client expects.
  - Ask questions about scope, deliverables, timelines, and success criteria.
  - Clarify whether the work is fixed-price or hourly.

- **Know your value and limits**
  *Expected result:* You negotiate with confidence.
  - Be clear on your minimum acceptable rate.
  - Consider your experience, skill level, and project complexity.

- **Discuss pricing openly**
  *Expected result:* Transparent and fair compensation terms.
  - Explain how your rate aligns with the value you deliver.
  - If needed, justify your rate using experience, results, or verified skills.

- **Define scope and deliverables clearly**
  *Expected result:* Reduced risk of misunderstandings.
  - Specify what is included and what is not.
  - Break the project into milestones if possible.

- **Agree on timelines and availability**
  *Expected result:* Realistic deadlines for both parties.
  - Confirm start dates, deadlines, and review cycles.
  - Be honest about your availability and response times.

- **Confirm revision and change terms**
  *Expected result:* Protection against scope creep.
  - Agree on how many revisions are included.
  - Define how additional work will be handled and billed.

- **Document the agreed terms**
  *Expected result:* Clear reference point for both parties.
  - Summarize agreed terms in writing before starting work.
  - Ensure both you and the client confirm the agreement.

## FAQ

**When should I negotiate terms?**
Before starting any work. Never assume details are understood without agreement.

**What if a client pushes for a lower rate?**
You can explain your value, adjust scope, or politely decline if it doesn't meet your minimum.

**Is it okay to say no to a project?**
Yes. Declining unfair terms protects your long-term growth and reputation.

**Should I negotiate everything?**
Focus on key areas: scope, pricing, timelines, and revisions.

**Does having verified skills help in negotiation?**
Yes. Verified skills and strong reviews increase trust and support higher rates.

## Troubleshooting

- **The client keeps changing requirements**
  Fix: Reconfirm scope and explain how changes affect pricing or timelines.

- **The client avoids discussing budget**
  Fix: Ask directly about budget range before committing.

- **I feel pressured to accept unfavorable terms**
  Fix: Take time to respond and revisit your priorities and limits.

- **The agreement feels unclear**
  Fix: Ask for clarification and restate terms in writing.

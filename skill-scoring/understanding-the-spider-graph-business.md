# Understanding the Spider Graph

**Who this is for:** Businesses evaluating Freelancers skill profiles

## What you'll achieve

- Learn how to read and interpret the spider (radar) graph on Freelancers profiles
- Understand how skill points translate into visual indicators of proficiency
- Use the graph to assess a candidate's strengths and fit for your project

## Prerequisites

- Freelancers profiles must have skills added with assigned points
- At least one skill is required for the graph to provide meaningful insights

## What the Spider Graph Represents

The spider graph is a visual representation of a Freelancer's proficiency across multiple skills. Each axis represents a specific skill (e.g., Frontend, Backend, APIs, Databases). The distance of the point from the center shows the Freelancer's proficiency level in that skill. The overall shape illustrates whether the Freelancer has a balanced skill set or specializes in certain areas.

**In simple terms:** More points mean the graph extends farther from the center, indicating higher skill proficiency.

## How to Read the Spider Graph

### Skill Axes

Each spoke corresponds to a particular skill listed around the graph. Helps you quickly identify which skills are evaluated.

### Distance from Center

Points closer to the center represent lower proficiency. Points farther out indicate higher expertise.

### Proficiency Levels

The graph may use colors or shading to differentiate skill levels such as Learning, Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, and Expert. These visual cues help you compare skills at a glance.

### Overall Shape

A rounded, even shape suggests the Freelancer has a broad, well-rounded skill set. A spiked shape indicates strong specialization in specific skills.

### Summary Indicators

Total number of skills displayed below the graph shows the Freelancer's skill coverage. The expert level count reflects how many skills the Freelancer has mastered.

## How Skill Points Affect the Graph

Skill points control how far each point extends along its axis. Higher points push the graph outward, highlighting strengths. Lower points pull the graph closer to the center, showing areas with less proficiency. The graph updates in real time as the Freelancer's skill points change.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is a spider graph?**
A circular chart that visualizes multiple skill proficiencies on a single graph for easy comparison.

**Does the graph update automatically?**
Yes, it reflects any updates or changes in the Freelancer's skill points immediately.

**Can a Freelancer have many low-level skills instead of few high-level ones?**
Yes, the graph shows either breadth (many skills) or depth (specialized skills) based on how points are assigned.

**What does a small or nearly empty graph indicate?**
The Freelancer has few skills listed or low proficiency points assigned.

**Do the colors affect scoring?**
No, colors are only visual indicators of proficiency levels; the actual scoring depends on the points assigned.

**Is a balanced graph better than a specialized one?**
It depends on your project needs -- balanced skills suit generalist roles, while spikes indicate niche expertise.

## Troubleshooting

- **Graph looks empty or very small:** The Freelancer may need to add more skills or increase points for better clarity.
- **Skill missing from the graph:** Confirm the skill is listed and saved on the Freelancer's profile.
- **Graph not updating:** Refresh the page and verify the Freelancer's profile changes.
- **Proficiency colors don't match expectations:** Check the proficiency thresholds used for color coding.

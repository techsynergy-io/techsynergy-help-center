# How Skill Scoring Works (100-Point System)

**Who this is for:** Freelancers

## What you'll achieve

- Understand how your skills are scored on a 100-point scale
- Know what each skill level means and how your experience translates into a score

## Prerequisites

- An active Freelancers profile
- At least one skill added to your profile
- Accurate information about your experience level (years of practice)

## How the skill scoring system works

The skill scoring system uses a **100-point scale** to clearly show your level of proficiency in any skill. Each skill level has a **multiplier**, which converts your experience into a percentage score out of 100.

The higher your level, the higher your multiplier — and the closer your score is to 100.

### Skill levels and multipliers

| Level | Multiplier | Percentage Score | What it means | Experience range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Learning | 0.2 | 20% | Just getting started | 0-6 months |
| Beginner | 0.4 | 40% | Basic understanding | 6 months - 1.5 years |
| Intermediate | 0.6 | 60% | Comfortable working | 1.5 - 3 years |
| Advanced | 0.8 | 80% | Very proficient | 3 - 5 years |
| Expert | 1.0 | 100% | Master level | 5+ years |

## Steps: How your score is calculated

1. **Select your skill level**
   Choose the level that best matches your current ability and experience.
   *Expected result:* Your skill is mapped to a predefined multiplier.

2. **Multiplier is applied**
   Each level has a multiplier between **0.2 and 1.0**.
   *Expected result:* Your experience is converted into a percentage value.

3. **Score is calculated on a 100-point scale**
   The multiplier is applied to the maximum score (100).
   **Example:** If you select *Intermediate (0.6)* -> 0.6 x 100 = **60 points**
   *Expected result:* Your skill displays a clear, standardized score.

4. **Score appears on your profile**
   Your final score helps clients quickly understand your proficiency.
   *Expected result:* Improved visibility and trust with potential clients.

## Why this matters

- Clients can easily compare skills using a standard scale
- Your experience is represented clearly and fairly
- It encourages honest self-assessment and skill growth

## FAQ

**1. Is the skill score based only on years of experience?**
No. Years of experience guide the level, but you should also consider your practical ability.

**2. Can I change my skill level later?**
Yes. You can update your skill level anytime as you gain more experience.

**3. Does a higher score guarantee I'll get hired?**
No, but it increases trust and makes your profile easier to evaluate.

**4. Why is the maximum score 100?**
The 100-point system is simple, universal, and easy for clients to understand.

**5. What if I overestimate my skill level?**
Clients may test your ability during interviews or tasks, so honesty is recommended.

**6. Can multiple skills have different scores?**
Yes. Each skill is scored independently based on its own level.

## Troubleshooting

**My score looks too low**

- Double-check that you selected the correct level
- Update your experience range if needed

**I can't edit my skill level**

- Ensure you're logged in
- Check that your profile is not under review

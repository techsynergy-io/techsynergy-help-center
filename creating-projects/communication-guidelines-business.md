# Platform Communication Guidelines

**Who this is for:** Businesses

## What you'll achieve

- Maintain professional, secure communication with Freelancer
- Protect Freelancers privacy and ensure project integrity

## Prerequisites

- An active Business account
- Access to platform messaging and notifications

## Guidelines for Businesses

- **Communicate exclusively through TechSynergy** *Expected result:* Secure and traceable communication.
  - Do not contact Freelancer via email, phone, or social media.
  - Keep all project discussions, agreements, and feedback on-platform.
- **Maintain professional tone and clarity** *Expected result:* Respectful and efficient collaboration.
  - Avoid offensive, slang, or unprofessional language.
  - Focus messages on project requirements, timelines, and deliverables.
- **Respect Freelancers privacy** *Expected result:* Builds trust and protects personal data.
  - Never request personal contact details.
  - All negotiations and communications must happen within the platform.
- **Document project discussions** *Expected result:* Clear reference points and reduced misunderstandings.
  - Keep messages focused on scope, pricing, milestones, and revisions.
  - Use platform tools for attachments and work updates rather than email.

## FAQ

**Can I ask Freelancer for their phone number or email?** No. All communication must remain on TechSynergy for safety and accountability.

**What if a Freelancers refuses to respond promptly?** Use the platform tools to send reminders or follow-up messages.

**Are informal messages allowed?** Keep all communication professional and relevant to the project.

**How do I report inappropriate messages from Freelancers?** Use the platform's reporting tool to flag issues to support.

## Troubleshooting

- **A Freelancers asks to communicate outside the platform** Fix: Politely refuse and remind them of TechSynergy policies.
- **I receive unclear project updates** Fix: Request clarification via on-platform messages and document agreements.
- **I'm unsure if a message is professional** Fix: Review before sending; keep tone factual and project-focused.
